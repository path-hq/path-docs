---
title: "Partner access"
description: "Path's intelligence layer is available to institutional partners on request."
---

Path's API is private. We do not publish open endpoints, rate-limited
public keys, or self-serve developer tooling. Access is granted to
institutional partners on a per-engagement basis after a partnership
conversation.

## Who this is for

Partner access is designed for:

- **Custodial earn surfaces** embedding Path's intelligence inside an
  existing institutional product.
- **Modular rail layers** integrating Path as a signal-driven
  strategy manager.
- **Asset managers** consuming the prediction stream alongside their
  internal allocation models.
- **On-chain vault contracts** consuming verifiable signed signals
  for autonomous allocation logic.

## Integration patterns

When we open a partner engagement, we work with the partner to pick
the integration shape that fits their stack:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Widget embed" icon="rectangle-list">
    A drop-in surface that renders Path's allocation intelligence
    inside an existing earn or treasury product. Reads the
    connected wallet context, presents the prediction, allocation
    suggestion, and break-even panel. Styles inherit from the
    embedding partner's design system.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Signal feed pull" icon="rss">
    A server-to-server prediction feed for partners who want to
    consume Path's output programmatically inside their own backend.
    Useful for partners running their own allocation logic that
    augments Path's signal with internal risk caps and policy rules.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Custodian webhook" icon="bell">
    An event-driven push surface for custodians who want to receive
    rebalance triggers and regime-change notifications inline with
    their existing operational tooling.
  </Card>
  <Card title="DON-signed signals" icon="signature">
    For partners that require on-chain verifiability rather than
    off-chain API trust, Path publishes its prediction stream through
    a Chainlink Runtime Environment workflow. Vault contracts read
    cryptographically signed signals on-chain.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How to start

Email **path@pathprotocol.finance** with:

- A short description of the partner surface you operate.
- The integration shape that fits best (widget, feed pull, webhook,
  or signed signal).
- The jurisdictions and protocols you care about.

We respond inside one business day, scope the engagement, and open
sandbox access after the partnership terms are agreed. We do not
publish pricing publicly; commercial terms are set inside each
partnership conversation.

This page is for the technical team on the other side of a partnership
conversation. The goal is to let a PM or DeFi lead navigate from "what
is Path" to "how would we plug Path into our existing rail" in a single
read.

## At a glance

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="What Path is" icon="brain">
    A prediction engine plus an embeddable surface. Signal Mesh ingests
    ~117 signals every 15 minutes and emits confidence-scored,
    multi-horizon predictions per pool. The intelligence drives
    automated allocation logic in later phases; today it is read-only.
  </Card>
  <Card title="What Path is not" icon="ban">
    Not a fund. Not advisory-only. Not a yield aggregator that chases

  <Card title="Where Path slots in" icon="puzzle-piece">

    Path runs the allocation strategy; the rail handles custody,
    execution, NAV reporting, KYC, and distribution.
  </Card>
  <Card title="How partners consume Path" icon="code">

    drop-in widget that embeds the prediction and allocation surface
    inside an existing earn product.

## Strategy-manager framing

Path's wedge into a modular rail is the **strategy-manager slot**, not
a yield-source slot. Concretely:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What Path contributes">
    A signal-driven allocation policy: which yield sources to weight,
    when to rebalance, with what conviction. Every recommendation
    carries a confidence score and the SHAP-style explainability
    behind it. Path scores ~296 pools every 15 minutes and emits
    predictions across 6h, 1d, and 30d horizons plus a continuous
    7-day APY estimator.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What the rail contributes">
    Custody, on-chain execution, NAV computation, KYC and
    jurisdictional wrapping, distribution to end-clients. Path does
    not aspire to replicate this layer; it is a strict separation of
    concerns and it is what makes the strategy-manager pattern work.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Why this works">
    The pattern matches the curator-strategy-manager shape that has
    driven institutional curator growth on-chain over the last
    18 months. The rail focuses on what it is good at (security,
    custody, regulatory wrapping); the strategy provider focuses on
    intelligence and allocation; both layers benefit from the network
    effect of shared user surfaces.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Concrete integration questions

The honest list of questions a PM should ask us in the first call.
Most have answers in the relevant section; a few are open and we will
say so.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Where do allocation decisions originate?">
    In Phase 1, Path publishes signal; the user decides. In Phase 2,
    Path crafts the rebalance transaction and an authorised signer
    (an asset manager wallet, an operator key, a partner contract)
    submits it. In Phase 3, a policy engine signs against a
    user-defined risk profile within partner-set guardrails. The phase
    the integration sits in is a contract decision per partner.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can we drive allocation from your API instead of from your UI?">
    Yes. `api.pathprotocol.finance/v1` exposes the prediction stream,
    the liquidity-impact context, the historical accuracy registry,
    and the rebalance-cost estimator. Authentication is API-key based
    for read endpoints; the partner can poll or subscribe.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can we embed your UI instead of building our own?">
    Yes. The widget reads the connected wallet, calls the Path API,
    and renders the allocation breakdown, the recommended rebalance
    with confidence and break-even, and the liquidity-impact context.
    The widget is design-system aware: pass colour tokens, typography
    tokens, and a layout container, and the widget styles itself to
    match.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do you support on-chain verifiable signal output?">
    Yes. Path's prediction stream runs through a Chainlink Runtime
    Environment (CRE) workflow that signs the prediction tuple
    `(pool_id, direction, confidence, predicted_apy, predicted_at)`.
    Any vault contract on any supported chain can read the signed
    signal with cryptographic guarantees. This converts off-chain
    compute trust into on-chain verifiability without requiring Path
    to operate its own validator set.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What is your model accuracy honestly?">
    Three bands, and we publish all three. Test-set sign-based
    accuracy is in the high 60s for the 1-day directional model.
    Live full-universe sign-based accuracy currently sits in the low
    50s. Live carve-out high-confidence (the operational range a
    strategy manager would actually trade) lands around 59.5% vs the
    DeFiLlama random-walk baseline of 53.5%, statistically significant
    at p around 0.0001. Per-protocol specialists score in the low 80s
    on their own protocol family. The published research ceiling on
    stablecoin 1-day directional classifiers is around 71-72%; that is
    the band we are targeting, not 90%.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens to predicted APY if I deposit at size?">
    The liquidity-impact context block on every prediction answers
    this directly. For a hypothetical deposit of size X, we compute
    pool-share representation, post-deposit APY via the protocol's
    actual rate mechanism (utilisation curve for lending, swap-fee
    math for AMM-style yield, governance-set rate for stability
    rails), expected slippage band where applicable, and a break-even
    horizon combining direction confidence, post-deposit APY, and gas
    cost. The post-deposit APY estimate matches realised within
    around 1 percentage point in 6 cases out of 10 on backtest data.
    See [Liquidity impact](/core-concepts/liquidity-impact) for the
    full surface.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What is your risk model?">
    Native, built in-house. Sanctions screening runs daily against
    Open Sanctions data plus a commercial provider's API, applied at
    pool level (associated treasury and audit-history addresses) and
    at allocator level (the wallet about to receive the
    recommendation). Pool-level risk combines smart-contract risk
    scoring, oracle staleness checks, governance-action history, and
    a native pool-jurisdiction scanner that validates against the
    embedding partner's incorporation thresholds.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What is the regulatory shape?">
    Phase 1 (today) is read-only information; no transactions, no
    custody, no managed-product framing. Zero regulatory surface.
    Phase 2 (transaction crafting) introduces a regulatory dependency
    that we structure against per-partner because the licensing
    obligation depends on the embedding partner's jurisdiction.
    Phase 3 (policy automation) requires the framework Phase 2 forces
    us into. We engage the regulatory framework inside each
    integration conversation, not by committing to one global
    structure.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do you handle drift?">
    A continuous-loop verifier daemon re-computes every accuracy
    number shown on any Path surface from canonical SQL every 30
    minutes. Drift greater than 0.5 percentage points pages our
    on-call and auto-files a ticket. The directional models retrain
    on a three-day cadence; a live-accuracy gate on every active
    model runs every 10 minutes and auto-deprecates any model that
    falls below the DeFiLlama baseline on a 100+ sample window.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What about team and bus-factor?">
    Path is operated by a small team augmented by a specialised agent
    crew (research, QA, content, operations) with full audit trails
    across the development and verification pipeline. The depth of
    automation around verification (continuous-loop verifier daemon,
    auto-deprecation gates, retrain-and-rollback policy) is
    deliberately high; the operational risk a partner inherits is
    bounded by these layers, not by raw headcount.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What we ask of an integration partner

The minimum contract for a sandbox or pilot integration:

- A clear scope (which strategy slot, which user cohort, which yield
  sources are in scope).
- A specification for how Path's output surfaces to the end-user
  (embedded widget, native UI consuming the API, or DON-signed signal
  read on-chain by a vault contract).
- A rebalance frequency window we should design Signal Mesh's signal
  emission cadence around.
- A jurisdictional posture so we can scope the regulatory framework
  per partner.

We do not require:

- An SDK lock-in. The widget and the API are independent surfaces; a
  partner can consume either or both.
- A revenue minimum. Pricing is a curator-fee shape (% of TVL or % of
  net yield over baseline); the right model is negotiated per partner.
- Exclusivity. Path runs as a strategy provider across multiple
  rails; we do not constrain partner distribution.

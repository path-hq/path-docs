This page captures the state of Kiln/Railnet's published documentation
as of 2026-05-15, cross-references it against the internal Railnet
onboarding spec, and lists the next implementable actions.

Source map for the upstream surface: `docs.railnet.org/llms.txt`
(complete documentation index) plus the per-section pages listed
below.

## What is published upstream

Railnet now ships full developer documentation at **docs.railnet.org**.
The architecture is in three composable layers connected by **STEAM**
(State Transition Engine for Asset Management), each with a documented
contract surface.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Layer 1: Yield Sources (Vehicles)">
    Smart-contract adapters wrapping each yield protocol. Native
    Vehicle adapters are live on Base testnet for Aave V3, Compound V3,
    Morpho Blue, and a generic ERC-4626 wrapper. Sync flows for
    instant settle, async flows for cooldown-style sources. Ethena
    (sUSDe) and Syrup (Maple Finance) are in-progress with async
    cooldown and queue withdrawals respectively. Pendle, Lido, Rocket
    Pool, EigenLayer, Symbiotic, and Karak are listed as planned
    custom adapter candidates. Roughly 28 additional protocols are
    flagged as ERC-4626 compatible and theoretically supported via
    the generic adapter without custom code. Source:
    `docs.railnet.org/developers/vehicles/supported-protocols.md`.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Layer 2: Strategies (Allocation + Advanced)">
    The strategy-manager slot. Two flavours documented:

    **Allocation Strategy** (MultiVehicle): a strategy manager
    deploys a five-contract bundle (MultiVehicle, Sector Accounting
    Engine, Queue Strategy Engine, Sub Query Engine, Query Redeem
    Queue) via a single factory transaction. Rebalancing is a single
    atomic call to `accounting.rebalance(sourceVehicle,
    targetVehicle, shareAmount)` requiring the
    `MULTI_VEHICLE_REBALANCE` role scoped to the Sector Accounting
    Engine. The operator owns capital allocation decisions and
    rebalance execution; the platform owns yield-source authorisation
    and fee structure.

    **Advanced Strategy**: a standalone strategy container with a
    7540 vault for investor lifecycle, a non-custodial strategy
    contract for execution, a policy engine for on-chain
    governance, and standardised NAV reporting. Source:
    `docs.railnet.org/strategies/advanced/index.md` and
    `docs.railnet.org/strategies/allocation/create.md`.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Layer 3: Conduits (Distribution wrappers)">
    Branding and fee-customisation layer on top of any Strategy.
    Lets a partner platform issue its own ERC-20 share token, attach
    its fee structure, and gate access via AccountList allow/blocklists
    (with sanctions oracle hooks). The Conduit pattern is how a
    platform like CoinShares ships a branded vault on top of a
    strategy. Source: `docs.railnet.org/conduits/index.md`.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Policy engine (Zodiac Roles Modifier v2)">
    On-chain governance enforcing operational constraints per
    Advanced Strategy. Four permission layers cover contract
    whitelists, function-level permissions, parameter-level checks,
    and timelock guardian governance. Default-disabled operations
    (`liquidate`, admin ops) need explicit opt-in; default-enabled
    ops (`withdraw`, `repay`, `claimWithdrawals`) execute on policy
    pass; medium-risk ops (`supply`, `borrow`, `swap`) are
    spending-capped. Important: **no built-in external signal
    integration**. The policy engine validates operator-crafted
    transactions; it does not ingest external feeds. Source:
    `docs.railnet.org/strategies/advanced/policy-engine.md`.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="GraphQL API (testnet)">
    Single endpoint at
    `query.railnet-testnet.defi.testnet.kiln.fi/v1/graphql` for
    portfolio, position, transaction-history, and protocol-state
    queries. Subscriptions documented. Authentication scheme and
    rate-limits are not yet published. Source:
    `docs.railnet.org/developers/api.md`. Note: the
    `api-reference/openapi.json` linked from the docs sidebar is still
    a Mintlify Plant Store placeholder; the production OpenAPI spec
    has not yet been published.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Reporting API (docs.api.railnet.org)">
    Referenced from `docs.railnet.org/developers/integrate-advanced-strategy.md`
    as the canonical Reporting API surface for partner integrations.
    Async deposits and redemptions flow through this surface
    (`requestDeposit` then poll `/positions` API to claim). The
    public landing page at `docs.api.railnet.org` did not return
    endpoint detail to our fetcher on 2026-05-15; the surface exists
    but the public navigation is still light.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Risk + compliance hooks">
    AccountList supports allowlists, blocklists, and sanctions oracle
    connections at the Conduit layer. Sector-based accounting tracks
    idle, deployed, in-flight, and pending-redemption capital in
    real-time per yield source. Error isolation prevents one yield
    source's failure from propagating to others. Source:
    `docs.railnet.org/strategies/risk-management.md`.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What Path has implemented today

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Path-side intelligence (live)">
    Signal Mesh combines on-chain protocol health, prediction market
    data, and macro signals every 15 minutes across Aave V3, Aave V4
    (multi-hub), Morpho Blue, Compound V3 (USDC and USDT), and Pendle
    on Ethereum, plus Aave V3, Morpho Blue, Compound V3, Aerodrome,
    and Moonwell on Base behind a feature flag. The directional model
    delivers around 78% accuracy on the high-conviction calls Path
    acts on (last 14 days, n=808) vs the DeFiLlama 53.5% baseline,
    with a Pendle protocol-family specialist scoring higher on its
    own family. Per-pool liquidity-impact context. SHAP explainability
    per recommendation.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Path-side surface (live)">
    Web app at app.pathprotocol.finance with the opportunity page,
    per-pool detail panels, and a connected-wallet read-only flow.
    REST and GraphQL API at api.pathprotocol.finance/v1 covering
    pools, predictions, briefing, and backtest endpoints. Docs at
    docs.pathprotocol.finance with the content pages and the
    integration onboarding flow.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Railnet-facing scope (in flight)">
    Internal onboarding spec captures the open Q&A gates from the
    2026-05-13 Dan Biton discovery call, of which one is fully
    answered (Salik Kogan and Tal Zerkone Fireblocks intros), four
    are partially answered (API+widget integration, vehicle expansion
    list, auto-NAV, Signal Mesh UI embedding), and the remainder are
    routed to the Darshan/Martin follow-up call. A Signal Mesh on
    Chainlink CRE Sepolia POC is the on-chain verifiable-signal
    track; an RWA sleeve scoping effort covers the BUIDL/ACRED side.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Path-side adapters NOT yet built">
    Path does not currently emit Railnet-compatible STEAM
    `create -> process -> settle` payloads. Path does not currently
    sign rebalance recommendations into the format an Allocation
    Strategy's `accounting.rebalance(...)` call expects. Path does
    not currently consume Railnet's Reporting API for partner-side
    NAV cross-validation. The on-chain verifiable signal track
    (CRE Sepolia POC) is in-review but not yet wired into a Railnet
    vault contract.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What to implement next

Ranked by leverage on the Dan Biton / Darshan / Martin conversation.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Testnet POC: Path-managed Allocation Strategy on Base">
    Smallest end-to-end proof. Deploy a MultiVehicle Allocation
    Strategy bundle via the documented factory pattern, weight three
    of the live Native Vehicle adapters (Aave V3, Compound V3,
    Morpho Blue), and wire `accounting.rebalance(...)` calls to
    Signal Mesh's daily recommended weights. End state: a public
    testnet contract demonstrating Path's allocation logic running
    inside a Railnet Allocation Strategy. Scope around two weeks of
    focused engineering.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reporting API client + NAV cross-validator">
    Thin client around `docs.api.railnet.org` to pull
    realised post-rebalance NAV for the testnet strategy and compare
    against Signal Mesh's predicted post-rebalance APY. This becomes
    the verifier daemon Path attaches to every Railnet integration:
    if Railnet-reported NAV drifts from Path-predicted, Path's
    accuracy publishing pipeline reflects it within 30 minutes.
    Scope around one week, blocked until the testnet POC above is
    live.
  </Step>
  <Step title="CRE Sepolia POC -> Railnet policy-engine integration">
    Once the Chainlink CRE Sepolia POC lands and publishes DON-signed
    prediction tuples, the next step is wiring a Path helper contract
    that consumes the signed tuple and is whitelisted by an Advanced
    Strategy policy engine as a permitted callee. Three-party
    coordination (Path + Kiln + Chainlink). This is the verifiable-
    signal follow-on once the CRE POC ships.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map Path's risk output into Railnet's AccountList feed">
    Path's native pool-jurisdiction scanner produces a pool-level
    allow/block determination per partner incorporation profile.
    Surface that determination via a feed that conforms to Railnet's
    AccountList sanctions-oracle interface so a Conduit operator can
    subscribe. Scope around one week, blocked on confirming the
    AccountList interface shape with Kiln in the Darshan/Martin call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Advocate Pendle adapter prioritisation in Kiln's roadmap">
    Pendle is the single highest-accuracy mechanism inside Signal
    Mesh on its own protocol family. Pendle is listed as a planned
    custom adapter in Railnet's supported-protocols doc. The
    conversation fits into the Darshan/Martin call. No engineering
    work needed; folds into the existing follow-up bundle.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Open questions for the Darshan/Martin call

These are the unknowns docs.railnet.org did not answer that we should
raise in the integration deep-dive Dan offered to organise:

- **Mainnet timeline.** All Native Vehicle adapters are live on Base
  testnet; the docs do not state a mainnet date.
- **Auth scheme for the GraphQL endpoint and the Reporting API.** Neither
  surface documents API-key or signed-request semantics. Confirm in the
  call.
- **Strategy Manager whitelisting process.** The Advanced Strategy
  integration doc says "Contact the Railnet team to be assigned a
  referral address before going live." What is the timeline and what
  does the diligence look like for a third-party strategy manager?
- **Allocation Strategy vs Advanced Strategy fit.** Path's profile fits
  Allocation Strategy more cleanly (multi-source weighting across the
  same protocol family). What is the upside of the Advanced Strategy
  surface for a partner whose value-add is allocation intelligence and
  not bespoke contract execution?
- **External-signal integration shape.** The policy engine validates
  but does not ingest. Is there a documented pattern for an external
  intelligence layer feeding allocation decisions, or is the expected
  shape "off-chain operator wallet signs the rebalance after reading
  the external signal"?
- **Conduit-side distribution mechanics.** Which of the named
  distribution channels (Ledger Live, Crypto.com, Trust Wallet,
  VanEck) consume Conduits programmatically vs require separate
  negotiation?

## Audit conclusion

Railnet's documentation surface is materially more advanced than what
existed at the time of the 2026-05-13 Dan Biton call. The Allocation
Strategy contract surface, the Conduit pattern, and the developer
docs at `docs.railnet.org` give Path a concrete integration target
that did not exist two days ago. The two priorities that should
unblock the Darshan/Martin call are (1) the testnet Allocation
Strategy POC and (2) the Reporting API NAV cross-validator. The
verifiable-signal track (CRE Sepolia + policy-engine whitelisting) is
the follow-on once the CRE POC ships.

Signal Mesh is Path's prediction engine. It ingests a wide cross-section
of on-chain, market, and macro signals on a continuous cadence, fans
them into a multi-horizon model stack, and emits confidence-scored
predictions per pool.

## Model architecture

Signal Mesh is a **multi-horizon ensemble of gradient-boosted
classifiers**, not a single monolithic model. The ensemble combines
short-horizon directional heads with longer-horizon stability and
continuous-yield estimators, each calibrated independently and
weighted by regime context at inference time.

The headline capabilities:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Multi-horizon ensemble">
    Predictions are published across multiple forward horizons in
    parallel: a short intraday directional view, a primary daily
    directional view, and a longer-horizon stability view. Each
    horizon answers a different allocation question and is scored
    independently against forward-resolved outcomes.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Regime detection">
    A dedicated regime-detection layer classifies the current market
    regime (trending, mean-reverting, dislocated, calm) and modulates
    the weights inside the ensemble accordingly. The same pool gets
    a different ensemble composition in a yield-spike regime than in
    a calm regime, which is what makes the multi-horizon view useful
    rather than noisy.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Confidence calibration">
    Every prediction is published with a calibrated confidence score
    on a 0 to 100 scale. The calibration step is what makes the
    confidence usable as an actionable threshold: a 90 means the
    model is meaningfully more sure than at 60, and a partner can
    act on that band difference. Uncalibrated raw classifier scores
    do not have this property.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="SHAP-style explainability">
    Every recommendation is shipped with a per-feature contribution
    breakdown using SHAP-style attribution. Partners can see which
    on-chain or macro signal drove the prediction up or down, not
    just the headline number. This is what makes Signal Mesh
    auditable rather than a black box.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Per-mechanism specialists">
    For protocol families where the underlying yield mechanism is
    structurally different from utilisation-curve lending (for
    example AMM-style yield), Signal Mesh routes to a dedicated
    per-mechanism specialist rather than forcing a single feature
    set across all protocols. The specialists materially outperform
    the generalist on their own protocol family.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Hybrid composite score">
    A hybrid composite combining the directional output, a continuous
    APY estimator, native risk signals, and liquidity-impact context.
    The composite score is what drives the front-page allocation
    ranking, not any single head in isolation.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Integrity behind the predictions

The model stack retrains on a regular cadence using rolling windows,
and every active model is gated by a live forward-accuracy monitor.
Models that drift below their published performance band are
automatically deprecated and flagged for retrain before any partner
surface continues to consume them. Calibration drift is one of the
few classes of failure that can ship silently in a model pipeline,
and we want that loop closed.

## How predictions surface

Every prediction is published with:

- The forward horizon
- A direction class (up, down, stable)
- A calibrated confidence score (0 to 100)
- A continuous APY estimate for the relevant horizon
- A SHAP-style feature attribution breakdown
- A liquidity-impact context: "you would represent X percent of this
  pool's TVL with a Y deposit"

The last item is the answer to the question every allocator asks
first: "if I move size, do I cannibalise the yield I am chasing?" It
is documented in detail under
[Liquidity impact](/core-concepts/liquidity-impact).

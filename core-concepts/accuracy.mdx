Path publishes three accuracy surfaces. Each answers a different
question; conflating them is how allocation decisions get distorted,
so they are reported separately and recomputed every 30 minutes from
canonical SQL.

## The three surfaces

<CardGroup cols={1}>
  <Card title="Test set" icon="flask">
    Cross-validated accuracy on the held-out evaluation slice from
    training. **Test-set sign-based accuracy lands in the high 60s**
    for the 1-day directional model, with per-mechanism specialists
    scoring in the low 80s on their own protocol family.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Live, full universe" icon="globe">
    Accuracy across every monitored pool, post-deployment, on
    forward-resolved data. The most conservative number and the right
    one for "how does the model behave in the wild".
    **Live full-universe sign-based accuracy sits in the low 50s** for
    the 1-day model.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Live, carve-out cohort, high confidence" icon="bullseye">
    Accuracy on the cohort of pools where Path has the deepest
    coverage, filtered to predictions where the model assigned
    85% or higher confidence. This is the operational range a
    strategy manager would actually trade.
    **Carve-out high-confidence sign-based accuracy: ~59.5%**, vs.
    the DeFiLlama random-walk baseline of 53.5%. The lift is
    statistically significant at p ~ 0.0001.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Integrity layer

A continuous-loop verifier daemon recomputes every accuracy number
shown on any Path surface (admin dashboards, public docs,
partner-facing exports) from the canonical SQL every 30 minutes.
Drift greater than 0.5 percentage points pages on-call and auto-files
a ticket. Every number on this site is the same number the
verifier last published.

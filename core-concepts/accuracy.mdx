Accuracy claims in DeFi are usually marketing. Path's approach is the
opposite: every prediction is published with a calibrated confidence
score and a per-feature attribution breakdown so that an allocator can
see not just *what* the model predicts but *how sure it is* and *why*.

## What confidence calibration means

A raw classifier score is not the same as a calibrated probability.
Two models can both output "0.85" and be wildly different in how often
they are right at that level. Signal Mesh runs a dedicated calibration
step on every active directional head so that the confidence score we
publish is interpretable as an actionable threshold:

- A prediction at confidence 90 is meaningfully more reliable than one
  at confidence 60.
- A partner can set a confidence floor (only act on predictions above
  threshold T) and trust that the threshold means something.
- Stratifying live performance by confidence band returns the
  monotonic shape calibration is supposed to deliver.

This is the property that makes the confidence score usable inside an
allocation policy rather than as a decorative number.

## SHAP-style explainability

Every prediction Path emits is accompanied by a per-feature
contribution breakdown using SHAP-style attribution. For any specific
recommendation, an allocator can see which on-chain signals,
prediction-market signals, macro indicators, or cross-protocol
correlations drove the directional call up or down.

This matters for two reasons:

1. **Auditability.** A risk officer can interrogate any single
   prediction and understand why the model said what it said. There
   is no black-box layer between the input signals and the output.
2. **Drift detection.** If the same protocol's prediction is suddenly
   being driven by a different feature group than it was last week,
   that is itself a signal that the regime has shifted, independent
   of the prediction's direction.

## Live monitoring

Path runs a continuous-loop verifier that re-computes every accuracy
band shown on partner-facing surfaces from canonical sources on a
short cadence. Performance bands that drift beyond a pre-set
tolerance auto-page on-call and gate the affected model out of the
live prediction stream. This is the operational hygiene behind every
performance band on this site: numbers that drift silently are how
trust gets destroyed in a single bad demo.

## What we publish, and what we do not

We publish:

- Calibrated confidence scores on every prediction.
- Per-feature attribution breakdowns on every prediction.
- Live performance bands on the partner dashboard for any partner
  with an active integration.

We do not publish:

- Detailed model architecture, training recipes, or feature
  engineering.
- Specific cohort breakdowns or per-pool win rates.
- Numbers that have not first cleared the live verifier.

The honest framing for an institutional partner is that Path is
opinionated about what it predicts, calibrated about how sure it is,
and transparent about why. The model recipe is proprietary; the
operational behaviour is not.

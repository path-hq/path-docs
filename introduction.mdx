## What Path is

Path is the intelligence layer for stablecoin yield. Companies and asset
managers deposit idle stablecoins, and our prediction engine—Signal
Mesh—tells them where the yield is likely to go, with what confidence, and
what the rebalancing cost of moving would be.

We do not chase the highest APY. We minimise net rebalancing friction and
maximise the net yield after gas and slippage. Most existing yield
aggregators are reactive and burn returns on excessive rebalancing; Path is
predictive and confidence-scored.

Path is a protocol with an intelligence layer. It is not a fund, and it is
not advisory-only. The intelligence drives automated allocation logic in
later phases; today it is read-only.

## The three-phase distribution shape

<Steps>
  <Step title="Phase 1 — Read-only intelligence (today)">
    Connect a wallet, view predictions across every monitored pool,
    drill into a specific pool to see directional signal with confidence
    score and a gas-aware break-even. The user decides; Path provides
    the signal. No transactions, no regulatory surface.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 2 — Transaction crafting (next)">
    Path generates the rebalance transactions; the user still signs and
    submits. Natural fit for modular rail layers and DON-signed
    verifiable-signal patterns where vault contracts read Path output
    directly with cryptographic guarantees.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 3 — Policy engine + automation">
    Policy engine that automates allocation based on a user-defined risk
    profile derived from on-chain behaviour analysis (yield-chaser vs.
    conservative). Requires the regulatory framework that Phase 2
    forces us into.
  </Step>
</Steps>

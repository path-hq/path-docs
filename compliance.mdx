Trust is the only product Path actually sells. Predictions are easy
to publish; predictions that institutional allocators can act on
require an integrity layer behind every number. This page documents
that layer.

## Regulatory posture

Path is an information layer. We do not custody funds and we do not
present allocation guidance as a managed product. The user remains
the decision-maker. Where a specific embedding requires transaction
crafting or signal-driven execution, the licensing route is scoped
per partner against the embedding partner's jurisdiction.

## Risk + screening stack

- **Sanctions screening** runs daily against Open Sanctions data plus
  a commercial provider's API, applied at the pool level (pool's
  associated treasury and audit-history addresses) and at the
  allocator level (the wallet about to receive the recommendation).
- **Pool-level risk** combines smart-contract risk scoring, oracle
  staleness checks, governance-action history, and a native risk
  model that scans pool jurisdiction against the embedding partner's
  incorporation thresholds.
- **Custodial integration** with a major institutional custody
  partner is in sandbox today.

## Verification

- Continuous-loop accuracy verifier (live today) re-computes every
  number on every Path surface from canonical SQL every 30 minutes.
  Drift greater than the published tolerance pages our on-call and
  auto-files a ticket.
- Third-party smart-contract audit is scoped against any deployment
  that takes custody or executes transactions.

## Data residency

Live prediction data is hosted on managed Postgres infrastructure
with encrypted backups, role-isolated access, and an audit log on
every read. Partner-specific data (wallet contexts, allocation
suggestions emitted to a specific partner) is segregated at the
schema level. Self-host is available for partners with strict
residency requirements.

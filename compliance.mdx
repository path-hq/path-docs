Trust is the only product Path actually sells. Predictions are easy to
publish; predictions that institutional allocators can act on require
a compliance layer that runs in front of every recommendation, not as
a post-hoc check. This page documents that layer.

## Compliance as a real-time gate

The defining property of Path's compliance stack is that it runs
**before** any pool enters the live allocation set. A pool that fails
any screen is not surfaced to a partner, not included in the
recommendation feed, and not part of the ranked output. Compliance is
a pre-condition for visibility, not a warning sticker attached to a
recommendation that already shipped.

Three checks run in parallel on every protocol and every pool we
monitor:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Address screening (Chainalysis)" icon="user-shield">
    Every protocol contract address, treasury address, and
    counterparty address is screened through Chainalysis against the
    standard institutional sanctions and risk feeds. Hits are
    blocked from the allocation set in real time and the screening
    result is logged against the protocol record for audit.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Smart contract risk scoring (GoPlus)" icon="shield-check">
    Every smart contract a pool touches is scored through GoPlus on
    contract-level risk signals: ownership concentration,
    upgradeability, hidden functions, mint authority, blacklist
    patterns, and on-chain incident history. Pools sitting on
    contracts with elevated risk scores are excluded from the
    allocation set or surfaced with a flagged status, depending on
    the partner's policy.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Pre-allocation gating" icon="gate-closed">
    The combined screening output gates a pool's eligibility for the
    allocation set on every refresh cycle. If a sanctions hit lands,
    if a contract upgrade introduces new risk, or if an incident
    flag fires on a counterparty, the pool is removed from the live
    set on the next cycle without waiting for human review.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Post-trade monitoring" icon="magnifying-glass">
    After a partner executes against Path's recommendation, the
    affected pool stays under continuous re-screening. Sanctions and
    risk-score state can change after a position is opened, and the
    monitoring loop flags any change so the partner can take
    appropriate action.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Why pre-allocation gating matters

The alternative pattern, in which compliance runs as a downstream
check after a recommendation has already been published, leaves a
gap that institutional allocators cannot accept. By the time the
post-hoc check fires, the recommendation has already been seen,
acted on, or surfaced inside an embedding partner's product. Path's
gating runs the other way around: the recommendation is not
emitted at all unless the compliance state is clean.

This is the property that makes Path consumable inside an
institutional allocation workflow rather than alongside one.

## Phase 1: no regulatory surface on top

Path Phase 1 is an information layer. We do not custody funds, we do
not execute transactions, and we do not present allocation guidance
as a managed product. The user remains the decision-maker. This is
the right starting point both for product reasons (wide
top-of-funnel) and for regulatory reasons (zero surface today, clean
runway to add it in Phase 2 with the appropriate framework). The
compliance gate described above runs regardless of phase because the
underlying screening discipline is the same whether the partner
consumes a signal or executes against it.

## Phase 2: regulatory dependency

Transaction crafting introduces a regulatory dependency that we are
structuring against deliberately. The licensing route depends on the
specific embedding partner's jurisdiction: a custodial earn surface
in one jurisdiction may carry a different obligation profile than a
modular rail layer in another. We engage on the regulatory framework
inside each integration conversation rather than committing to a
single global structure that will not fit every partner.

## Audit posture

- The continuous-loop verifier re-computes every performance band
  shown on Path surfaces from canonical sources on a short cadence,
  with drift beyond tolerance auto-paging on-call.
- Third-party smart-contract audit is scoped against the Phase 2
  transaction-crafting deployment.
- Partner-specific data (wallet contexts, allocation suggestions
  emitted to a specific partner) is segregated at the schema level
  with role-isolated access and a read audit log.

The framing for institutional partners is straightforward: the
compliance layer is the precondition for everything else Path
publishes. We do not separate "the intelligence" from "the
screening" because the screening is what makes the intelligence safe
to consume.

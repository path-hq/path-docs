Trust is the only product Path actually sells. Predictions are easy
to publish; predictions that institutional allocators can act on
require an integrity layer behind every number. This page documents
that layer.

## Phase 1 (read-only): no regulatory surface

Path Phase 1 is an information layer. We do not custody funds, we
do not execute transactions, and we do not present allocation
guidance as a managed product. The user remains the decision-maker.
This is the right starting point both for product reasons (wide
top-of-funnel) and for regulatory reasons (zero surface today,
clean runway to add it in Phase 2 with the appropriate framework).

## Phase 2 (transaction-crafting): regulatory dependency

Transaction crafting introduces a regulatory dependency that we are
structuring against deliberately. The licensing route depends on the
specific embedding partner's jurisdiction—a custodial earn surface
in one jurisdiction may carry a different obligation profile than a
modular rail layer in another. We engage on the regulatory framework
inside each integration conversation rather than committing to a
single global structure that will not fit every partner.

## Risk + screening stack

- **Sanctions screening** runs daily against Open Sanctions data plus
  a commercial provider's API, applied at the pool level (pool's
  associated treasury and audit-history addresses) and at the
  allocator level (the wallet about to receive the recommendation).
- **Pool-level risk** combines smart-contract risk scoring, oracle
  staleness checks, governance-action history, and a native risk
  model that scans pool jurisdiction against the embedding partner's
  incorporation thresholds.
- **Custodial integration** with a major institutional custody
  partner is in sandbox today; production roll-out is sequenced with
  the Phase 2 transaction-crafting work.

## Audit roadmap

- Continuous-loop accuracy verifier (live today) re-computes every
  number on every Path surface from canonical SQL every 30 minutes.
  Drift greater than the published tolerance pages our on-call and
  auto-files a ticket.
- Third-party smart-contract audit is scoped against the Phase 2
  transaction-crafting deployment. Not applicable to Phase 1
  read-only mode.
- Annual SOC 2 type II is on the post-Phase-2 roadmap.

## Data residency

Live prediction data is hosted on managed Postgres infrastructure
with encrypted backups, role-isolated access, and an audit log on
every read. Partner-specific data (wallet contexts, allocation
suggestions emitted to a specific partner) is segregated at the
schema level. Self-host is available for partners with strict
residency requirements.
